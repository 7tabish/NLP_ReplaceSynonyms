Consumers have been adopting exuberant digital technologies in their day-to-day lives at a rapid pace. Customers went from being express_mail to brick-and-mortar stores to researching, comparing and buying a company's products and services on-line. Along with those shifts came a change in how customers communicate with companies.

Today, no matter what channel customers use to interact with a brand -- phone, email, text, chat, on-line communities -- companies must be capable to create positive, consistent and engaging customer experiences.

Customer experience, or CX, is on the top of many CEOs' minds, research consistently shows. In fact, at a Holocene gathering of nearly 200 CEOs, a bracing definition of the purpose of a corporation emerged. Among the many things discussed at the meeting: Being internally focused on things like shareholder value is no thirster the priority it once was.
